## Overview

The **Cell Chip Group** at TU Wien develops **microfluidic and lab-on-a-chip systems** for life science applications.  
The group focuses on integrating **engineering, biology, and sensor technology** to build advanced biomedical platforms.

Core areas include:
- Lab-on-a-Chip systems
- Organ-on-a-Chip platforms
- Biosensing & diagnostics
- Microfluidic system integration

---

## Mission

- Develop **automated, miniaturized biological systems**
- Enable **precise and reproducible in vitro models**
- Support **next-generation diagnostics and drug testing**
- Contribute to **reduction of animal experiments**

---

## Research Areas

### 🧪 Lab-on-a-Chip & Microfluidics
- Microfluidic device design and fabrication  
- Fluid handling at micrometer scale  
- Integration of electronics and sensing  
- Rapid prototyping workflows  

---

### 🫀 Organ-on-a-Chip (OoC)

Development of **microphysiological systems** that replicate human tissue and organ function.

Focus:
- Controlled microenvironments for living cells  
- Mechanical, chemical, and biological stimulation  
- Long-term culture under physiologically relevant conditions  

Examples:
- Vascularized tissue chips  
- Gut-on-a-chip barrier models  
- Joint / synovium-on-chip systems  
- Multi-organ interaction platforms  

Applications:
- Drug screening  
- Disease modeling  
- Toxicology testing  
- Personalized medicine

## Technology Stack (Typical)

### Hardware
- Microfluidic chips (glass, silicon, polymer)  
- Integrated electrodes and sensors  
- Pumping systems (pressure / peristaltic)  

### Firmware
- Embedded systems (e.g., microcontrollers, sensor AFEs)  
- Real-time data acquisition  

### Software
- Data acquisition pipelines  
- Signal processing (e.g., impedance analysis)  
- Visualization dashboards  
- Machine learning for biological data
